Lesson Plan Example 1: Nursing 

Week 1

    1. Review syllabus and assignments
    2. Breakouts-muddiest points related to syllabus and assignments.
    3. Discussion question for class:
      • “Overdependence on lecture is indefensible in teaching material for a practice profession” (DeYoung, 2015, p. 59)
      • “Because nursing is a practice discipline, a lot of the learning should be active learning; that is, the learner should be an active participant in the learning process” (DeYoung, 2015, p. 59)
    4. Share your reflections on the theories/ theorists discussed this week (for example, what are some take-aways that you can use in your practice as a leader/ educator).
      • What are the two major categories of learning theorists?
      • What are strategies to take from the cognitivists?
    5. Break out activity:  Write three learning objectives on a topic of your choice at three different levels of Bloom's Taxonomy.  Identify the level of each objective.
    6. Show
      • Things nursing students say: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6S59PBZw1qE
      • My first clinical check off: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Lz2vT3T5hI
    7. Discussion question: Identify three take-aways from the Nurse Blake videos that inform your practice related to instructional strategies.

What's Great about this Lesson Plan

  • There's a review of the syllabus and assignments, but it doesn't take up the entirety of the class session.
  • There are a variety of active interactions planned, including structured discussion questions, application questions, breakout activities, and a muddiest point reflection. This will help students get used to a range of activities they can expect throughout the term.
  • Students will have ample opportunity to talk to one another and get to know each other on the first session.
  • The videos chosen are very short and are primarily used as jumping-off points for discussion.

 

 

Lesson Plan Example 2: Nursing 

Week 2

  • Scenario 1: You just agreed to teach a class on leadership in the RN to BSN program.
  • What teaching methods are you going to use?
    • Think/write, pair/share
      • 1 minute to think about this and write your ideas
      • 3-5 minutes to pair and share
      • 2 minutes to discuss as a class
  • Scenario 2: You just agreed to teach a class on “insulin administration and your work place’s policies” to new hires.
  • What teaching methods are you going to use?
    • Think/write, pair/share
      • 1 minute to think about this and write your ideas
      • 3-5 minutes to pair and share
      • 2 minutes to discuss as a class

What's Great about this Lesson Plan

  • Think-pair-share is a great way to help students warm up and to launch a discussion.
  • Both of the two main activities involve specific scenarios that students might encounter at their jobs, so they allow students to apply their learning to specific professional contexts.
  • This lesson plan provides times for each part of the activity, which will help both the instructor and the students stay on track.

 

 

Lesson Plan Example 3: Health Administration

Live Virtual Participation

Agenda

  • Class Q & A – 10  minutes (Welcome students. Ask about questions concerning content in weekly module. Remind students that their participation will be graded on proactive and regular contributions, their consistent preparation, and the ability to logically connect content to real life situations.)
  • Marketing within Healthcare Organizations Lecture Content – 25 minutes (Add content that is best delivered live.)
  • 4 Ps Group Activity in Healthcare (see: Marketing Mix Templates ) – 30 minutes (Divide students into Breakout rooms to discuss the topic, using the attached document. Then bring them back to report out.)
  • Marketing plan rubric review – Q&A – 20 minutes (Open discussion, full class)
  • Group Break-out session – Discussion questions – 20 minutes (Send students to breakout rooms to discuss questions provided within module.)
  • Class Wrap-up – the week ahead – 10 minutes

What's Great about this Lesson Plan

  • There is time allotted to explaining expectations in a transparent way, including looking over a rubric. Spending time talking about expectations really clarifies things for students and helps them to be successful, and using the live class session is a great time to do this.
  • A short lecture is included that gives the instructor time to share their own expertise. It could be a moment for the instructor to share how the course concepts relate to the professional world, and they might share stories or examples from their own professional life.
  • There are several activities for students to have a chance to work peer to peer.
  • There is intentionality in how the class begins and ends, with time dedicated to making that transitional period meaningful.

 

 

Lesson Plan Example 4: Health Administration

Communicate to students the expectations for the class session:

  • View the video linked to the Activities page
  • Read 2 articles on the Activities page
  • Be prepared to actively participate by answering questions about the materials

Agenda

  1. Q & A about weekly content (15 minutes)
  2. Open Discussion (with a Guiding Question) about the video (20 minutes)
  3. Discuss 1st article (Have questions prepared to direct to individual students) (20 minutes)
  4. Break students into groups and have them role play solutions to the issues presented in the 2nd article. (40 minutes)
      • Make groups with enough students to have the same number of roles in each group.
      • Assign the roles or have the students self-assign.
      • Provide instructions on how to report back to the whole group.
  5. Use the Muddiest Point activity to Wrap up the week. (15 minutes)

What's Great about this Lesson Plan

  • Students arrive ready to participate because they have been prepped about the expectations and the agenda.
  • There are a variety of different activities that let students interact with each and with the content in meaningful ways, and that encourage them to apply their learning in professional contexts.
  • The Muddiest Point activity makes good use of the precious last 15 minutes of class, which when used for reflection can have a big impact.
