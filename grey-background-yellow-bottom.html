Expectations for Training Completion

Once you are added to this course, you are expected, by the OLC and your department, to complete it within two (2) weeks and at least two (2) weeks before students are loaded. You will need this time to review the course content and assignments and make any necessary adjustments.

The content review should take about two hours of your time.

The final module, Completion of Training, will only open after you have completed all of the module requirements.

Resources Available from the Library

Many of the ideas in this menu are adaptations of well-known classroom assessment techniques and activities. For more ideas, check out the following resources:

  • Angelo, T. A., & Cross, K. P. (1993). Classroom assessment techniques: A handbook for college teachers. San Francisco, CA: Jossey-Bass.
  • Barkley, E. (2010). Student engagement techniques: A handbook for college faculty. San Francisco, CA: Jossey-Bass. Available as an ebook from the Library here.
  • Barkley, E., Howell Major, C., & Cross, K. P. (2014). Collaborative learning techniques: A handbook for college faculty. San Francisco, CA: Jossey-Bass.

This menu is also heavily informed by theories of motivating adult learners, transformational teaching, and student engagement from these resources:

  • Slavich, G. M. & Zimbardo, P. G. (2012). Transformational teaching: Theoretical underpinnings, basic principles, and core methods. Educational Psychology Review 24(4), 569-608. Available as a PDF here.
  • Wlodkowski, R. J., & Ginsberg, M. B. (2010). Teaching intensive and accelerated courses: Instruction that motivates learning. San Francisco, CA: Jossey-Bass. Available as an ebook from the Library here.
  • Zepke, N., & Leach, L. (2010). Improving student engagement: Ten proposals for action. Active Learning in Higher Education 11(3), 167-177. Available as a PDF here.
